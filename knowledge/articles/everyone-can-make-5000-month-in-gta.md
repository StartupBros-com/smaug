Comprehensive breakdown of three income paths opened by Rockstar's 2023 acquisition of FiveM (the GTA modding platform) and the January 2026 launch of a paid Cfx Marketplace: selling Lua scripts, running whitelist RP servers with subscription memberships, and positioning for GTA 6's UGC economy launching November 2026. The central unlock is that Claude can write Lua from a plain-English description, removing the coding barrier that previously kept non-developers out.

## Key Takeaways

- Cfx Marketplace bundles sell up to $389.99; developers report €5,000+/month within 90 days via Tebex external stores
- Whitelist RP servers charge $15–$25/month per member; top servers run 500+ members ($12,500/month recurring)
- GTA 6 UGC platform is being built on the FiveM infrastructure; Rockstar has hired 4 Creator Platform engineers
- Creator math: if 1% of GTA 6 players spend $10/month on UGC, that's a $240M annual creator economy
- Roblox paid out $1B to creators in 2025; the top 10 averaged $33.9M each — GTA 6 targets an older, higher-income audience
- Strategy: build on FiveM now to develop skills, portfolio, and audience before GTA 6 launch
