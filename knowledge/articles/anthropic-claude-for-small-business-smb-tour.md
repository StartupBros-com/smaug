TechCrunch (Lucas Ropek, May 13 2026) covers Anthropic's push into the small business market via Claude for Small Business, framing the 36 million U.S. SMBs as the next major battleground in the AI platform wars — distinct from the Fortune 500 enterprise focus that has dominated AI go-to-market strategy.

Key details from the tweet context:
- Tenex Labs (@tenex_labs) is Anthropic's official AI transformation partner for the initiative
- Co-built "Cowork" plugin: 31 SMB-specific skills (payroll, closing the month, invoice chasing, campaign management)
- Claude SMB Tour: 10-city, free, half-day workshops for 100 SMB leaders per stop, starting in Chicago May 14 2026

Case study cited: Rick Chorney (29-year-old, high school dropout, janitorial company in Abbotsford BC):
- Year 1 revenue: $242K; Year 2: $1M; Year 3 projected: $1.3M
- Used AI receptionist ($99/month) to replace a $4K/month role
- AI email triage cut inbox management to 20 minutes/day
- Now works 8-hour days and takes vacations

Macro argument: SMBs represent 44% of U.S. GDP; a 10% profit lift across all SMBs would add $1.41 trillion to GDP. SMBs can adopt AI faster than enterprises due to absence of governance overhead and bureaucracy.
