A personal retrospective by HangukQuant marking two years of solo crypto quantitative trading and crossing $1M in PnL. The piece traces a progression from hand-executed funding arbitrage with spaghetti dashboards to building quantpylib (1000+ commits, still growing), then pivoting to high-frequency strategies after hitting scaling limits on low-vol arb.

The arc is as much psychological as technical. After early success at ~40% APR with ~10 Sharpe on funding arb, the author shut it down — too much capital risk for the vol, too much attention cost pulling him from compounding his own skills. A spiral of imposter syndrome followed, amplified by public skepticism on X and Reddit, culminating in anxiety and panic attacks. Recovery came from biasing toward action: daily pushups, cold showers, writing code before the darkness could catch up.

The HFT phase produced a new gold mine strategy that landed him consistently atop leaderboards, but that edge is now fading. The article ends with the Jim Simons line — "there's no such thing as the goose that lays the golden egg forever" — and a note that he is "dumb again, but with more iron."

Key through-lines: compound your own skills when young, fear is good marketing but don't be the product, and AI (Claude/OpenGod) is a tool for the adaptable, not a threat to them.

## Key Takeaways

- Funding arb at ~10 Sharpe is real but hard to scale without risking meaningful personal capital; the point diminishes returns hit surprisingly early
- HFT edges compound with engineering depth: C++, mathematical sharpening, and months of failed experiments preceded the working strategy
- Public presence while learning attracts unfair skepticism — responding by shipping more rather than defending is the winning move
- Mental health as a solo quant/founder is a first-class problem; the "bias toward action before paralysis" framework is simple but genuinely useful
- Open-sourcing quantpylib alongside trading created community leverage that monetized separately from the trading PnL itself
