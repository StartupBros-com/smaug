local.ai is a benchmarking site that measures how local AI models actually perform on real hardware. Rather than synthetic benchmarks, it focuses on agent workloads — measuring capability, speed, and energy consumption across every model, quantization level, and harness combination.

The site is positioned as the authoritative source for "real numbers" on local AI, covering the full matrix of model × quantization × hardware × harness. Launched around June 2026 as local AI became accessible enough to matter for everyday use.

## Key Takeaways

- Benchmarks are based on agent workloads, not synthetic tasks — reflects real-world performance
- Covers multiple dimensions: capability, tokens/second speed, and energy draw
- Compares across different quantization levels (important for fitting models on consumer hardware)
- Includes a sign-up for early access to the full dashboard
