Anh (anhtho), co-founder of Lago, shares the philosophy and mechanics behind her Claude writing skill, open-sourced as a template at github.com/getlago/inside-lago-voice-skill. She has hit the HN front page 10+ times, often with emotionally resonant technical posts. Her key insight: good writing requires a soul, not just taste. The skill was built by reverse-engineering the delta between raw AI drafts and what she actually sends — capturing her anti-patterns, structure preferences, and formatting rules. As a non-native English speaker, shorter sentences and directness became features, not bugs. The emotional authenticity cannot live in a Claude skill; the skill handles the mechanical repetition so she can focus on truth and voice.

## Key Takeaways

- Claude skill captures anti-patterns and structural preferences, not emotion — that part must come from the writer
- Build the skill from your own published work: identify what you always delete, how you rewrite AI drafts
- The delta between AI draft and final send is the voice — encode that delta in the skill
- Non-native English patterns (short sentences, directness) can be embraced as identity, not corrected away
- Open-sourced template: github.com/getlago/inside-lago-voice-skill — structure ready, you fill the fuel
