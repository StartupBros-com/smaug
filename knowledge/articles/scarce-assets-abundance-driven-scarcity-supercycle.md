Packy McCormick's thesis: the coming era of AI-driven abundance paradoxically creates a scarcity supercycle for physical and irreplaceable assets. As software, information, and computation become cheap and plentiful, the things that cannot be replicated — land, energy infrastructure, natural resources, elite human attention — become relatively more valuable.

## Key Takeaways

- Abundance in one domain (software/AI/compute) amplifies scarcity in adjacent domains (physical assets, energy, land)
- Assets with fixed or hard-to-scale supply will outperform as AI productivity floods the economy with cheap goods and services
- The "scarcity supercycle" framing positions irreproducible things as the natural hedge to AI deflation
