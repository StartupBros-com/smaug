A former discretionary trader turned quant argues that LLMs are dissolving the two historic barriers that kept quantitative strategies out of fuzzy, low-sample-size markets: (1) the inability to quantify unstructured or qualitative information, and (2) the need for statistical regularity across many historical instances. LLMs can now turn earnings call transcripts, legal documents, and founder social signals into structured, high-dimensional features at scale — and can generalize from N=1 events by reasoning at the level of underlying features rather than specific instances. The result is that asset classes previously exclusive to discretionary traders (pharma approvals, venture, macro narratives) are becoming tractable for systematic approaches.

## Key Takeaways

- Quantitative trading historically required both quantifiability and statistical regularity; LLMs erode both constraints simultaneously
- LLMs replace hordes of analysts doing sentiment/qualitative work with consistent, scalable, high-dimensional feature extraction
- Novel events (drug approval, startup outcome) are N=1 at the event level but not at the feature level — LLMs can generalize across feature combinations seen in analogous historical situations
- Data providers (Bloomberg, Refinitiv) will productize LLM-extracted structured features, lowering barriers further
- Discretionary traders with edge from pattern recognition on public information will be outcompeted; those with proprietary relationships or domain expertise retain a shrinking premium
