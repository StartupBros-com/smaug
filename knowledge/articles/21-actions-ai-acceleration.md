A practical playbook for people who have internalized that the world will look unrecognizable in 5-10 years due to AI. The author argues that if you genuinely believe in exponential AI takeoff, your present-day behavior should reflect that — across investing, finances, career choices, and lifestyle planning. The list deliberately challenges conventional wisdom (e.g., skip 401k contributions, take on long-dated fixed-rate debt, stop planning 30-40 years out).

## Key Takeaways

- Invest directly in AI proliferation (Nvidia, Tesla, Palantir, Google, Anthropic/OpenAI) and adjacent exponential industries (robotics, biotech, crypto, space)
- Avoid locking capital into 30-40 year retirement vehicles like 401ks if you're under 50 — prefer liquidity over multi-decade tax optimization
- Pull forward future cash flows: take on fixed-rate long-dated debt (mortgages, student loans) rather than paying it off early — the future debt will likely be irrelevant
- Don't start law school, med school, or other multi-year credential programs expecting financial ROI; plan for most knowledge work to be automated within 5 years
- Build distribution (audience, brand, leverage) instead of credentials; become a power user or builder of AI systems
- Stop planning your life with a 30-40 year time horizon; don't optimize for distant longevity or retirement-age quality of life
- Do your bucket list now; buy a house near family sooner rather than later
