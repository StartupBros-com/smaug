FLOODGATE partner Ann Miura-Ko shares observations from visiting AI-native SF startups over a month. The pattern: PM roles are disappearing into engineering, the biggest strategic risk is the feature factory (when you can build anything in a day, you want to build everything), and the tech stack has converged on Slack + Claude Code + GitHub + Linear. Cursor went from dominant to barely mentioned as Claude Code took over. The most underestimated shift is what AI does for non-engineers: an account manager automated her upload workflow in one hour, accounting teams write database queries via MCP, a Chief of Staff produces materials in 30 minutes. Speed compounds — companies iterate 3-5x faster, running experiments in parallel and covering unknowns at scale.

## Key Takeaways

- PM role is being absorbed into engineering at AI-native startups; only 1 full-time PM across 5 companies visited
- Feature factory is the dominant strategic risk when execution is nearly free — winners use hard constraints (JSON-only config, North Star metrics to kill ideas)
- Converged stack: Slack (agent orchestration layer), Claude Code, GitHub, Linear — Cursor largely displaced
- Non-engineers are becoming first-class builders: account managers, accountants, and chiefs of staff shipping real automation
- Companies simulate customers with AI personas before touching real users; 100s of research interviews per week instead of 50 per quarter
- Gap between AI-native and AI-debating companies is enormous and widening every week
